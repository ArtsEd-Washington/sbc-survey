Adna School District

Position Three

Daniel Holmes

Which of these arts did you have as a part of your own K-12 education?

Theater, Music, Media

How did the arts impact your education and life after high school?

Music helped give me self confidence and dedication.

Research has shown that arts education increases student engagement, helps close opportunity gaps, and reduces drop out rates, yet income, race, and geography continue to be barriers to access to the arts. How do you see yourself addressing fair access in your district?

I would identify specific issues and areas of potential growth and including those as part of a strategic plan for growth in the future. The school district has expanded faster than the facilities or programs offered.

If elected, which of these strategies would you consider to support arts education in your district?

  • Research current arts program and assess needs at district buildings
  • Work for a budget to financially support arts instruction at all schools, My main goal is to create a strategic plan for the district to guide it into the future. I would like that strategic plan to cover all potential and realized needs.

Position Two

Pamela Johnson

Which of these arts did you have as a part of your own K-12 education?

Visual Arts, Music

How did the arts impact your education and life after high school?

Arts continues to impact me as I value what I learned and continue to learn as the arts have taken on different forms with technology. Music has been a huge part of my family’s life and its cultivation is what is grounding me even as Arts is often one of the first program deleted from the schools. Art is needed and necessary even more so now than ever as it helps our students self identified themselves.

Olympia School District

Position Five

Don Mitchell

Which of these arts did you have as a part of your own K-12 education?

Music, Theater, Media

How did the arts impact your education and life after high school?

Participation in music and graphic arts gave me a greater appreciation for the creative arts, and an understanding of how diverse influences can be harmoniously melded.

Research has shown that arts education increases student engagement, helps close opportunity gaps, and reduces drop out rates, yet income, race, and geography continue to be barriers to access to the arts. How do you see yourself addressing fair access in your district?

We would first need to assess what the specific barriers are, then effectively address them. For example, if money for an instrument is the challenge, exploring using school funds to acquire instruments that can be loaned, or scholarships for students with need to be able to buy an instrument. Likewise, if transportation is an issue, exploring using supplemental school bus service if practice and/or performance require travel/time beyond what is normally available for students.

If elected, which of these strategies would you consider to support arts education in your district?

  • Research current arts program and assess needs at district buildings
  • Develop or update a district level arts plan
  • Work for a budget to financially support arts instruction at all schools
  • Expand district-wide data collection for tracking, support, and accountability
